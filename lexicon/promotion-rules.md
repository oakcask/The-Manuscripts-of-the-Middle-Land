# Lexicon Promotion Rules

These rules decide when a phrase should become a managed lexicon term instead of remaining ordinary prose.

## Promotion Rule

A phrase should be promoted to the lexicon when at least one is true:

- it recurs across myths or manuscripts
- its meaning differs between orthodox, heretical, voyager, funerary, child, or censored witnesses
- it hides a scientific, ethical, or theological pressure
- replacing it with a normal word would damage the myth
- translator notes need to warn readers about it

Do not promote every beautiful phrase. The lexicon is for dangerous or structurally important terms, not decoration.

## Where To Register

When promoting a phrase:

1. Add the term to `index.md`.
2. Add the relevant catalog entry to `terms.md`.
3. Add or expand a detail entry under `terms/` only when the term needs sustained semantic, manuscript, or translation management.

If a phrase is only locally beautiful and does not need recurring control, leave it in the prose and do not create lexicon overhead for it.
