## Story Planning

* Put short-story outlines, plot notes, scene plans, POV notes, ending variants, and draft scaffolds in `notes/stories/`.
* Keep `stories/` for actual prose drafts, not planning notes.
* Keep anthology-level story seed lists in `notes/04-story-seeds.md` or move them to `notes/stories/index.md` when the list becomes active planning material.
* The bilingual-output rules do not apply to planning notes in `notes/stories/`.

## Story Titles

* Do not default to naming a short story after the corresponding myth chapter.
* Prefer a title that reflects the story's concrete content, loss, danger, evidence, or moral pressure.
* Alternatively, use a sentence or phrase from the myth that strongly suggests the story's central event.
* Track working titles and title rationale in the corresponding planning note under `notes/stories/`.

## Story Plotting

* When creating a short-story plot, do not default to a single narrator or single canonical viewpoint.
* Create multiple plot variants from distinct narrating positions, ideally tied to different manuscript traditions or interpretive stances.
* Each viewpoint variant should change what the story treats as the central loss, danger, evidence, or moral pressure, not merely retell the same plot with a different narrator.
* Keep the shared event core visible, but leave the final truth unresolved so the variants can become source material for multiple myth manuscripts.

## Writing Outputs

* Apply these bilingual-output rules only to prose writing deliverables in `myths/` and `translations/`.
* For those deliverables, create the English version first as `*.en.md`.
* Create the matching Japanese version as `*.ja.md` in the same location.
* Treat the English version as the primary drafting text, because resolving ambiguity after drafting first in Japanese makes later English translation much harder.
* Keep both language versions aligned in structure, scene order, terminology choices, and notes unless the user explicitly asks for a language-specific adaptation.
* When revising Japanese myths, translations, or stories, follow `lexicon/style-guardrails.md` for lexical clarity. Do not preserve an English ambiguity by literal translation when the Japanese phrase becomes opaque.
