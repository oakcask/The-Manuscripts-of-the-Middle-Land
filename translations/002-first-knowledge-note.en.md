# Translator's Note on the First Knowledge

I have rendered the central term as First Knowledge because the First Manuscript requires a phrase that can be spoken in communal worship without immediate accusation. This choice is conservative. It may also be evasive.

Knowledge suggests comprehension after the fact. The source term may not permit that comfort. In several aligned passages, the act of knowing appears to alter the thing known. A more exact but less readable rendering might be first determination, first fixing, or first unrecoverable seeing. I have not adopted those phrases in the myth body because they would force the later technical suspicion into the communal voice too early.

The watchers of the rim are therefore difficult to translate. If they merely observed an already broken sky, they are witnesses. If their watching fixed what had remained otherwise unsettled, they are participants. If the distinction between witness and participant belongs only to the receiving language, then my translation has already falsified the event by distributing guilt among nouns that the source did not separate.

The later term watching sin is not used in the approved surface of this chapter. Its absence should not be read as innocence. The First Manuscript often avoids a dangerous term by surrounding the place where it would have appeared with bread, water, ash, song, and counsel. I have preserved that avoidance. I cannot prove that I have not repeated it.

The Creator is more troubling here than in the ship-making chapter. The people ask whether the Creator moved the lamps, but the chapter does not let the question mature into doctrine. The omission may be pious restraint. It may be censorship. It may also mean that no agent stood behind the event, and that later readers supplied one because an irreversible change without intention is harder to mourn.

I should add that my English phrase act of attention is already too clean. Attention may imply a mind. The source may indicate an operation with no inwardness, or with inwardness distributed beyond recognition. I retain knowledge because the receiving tradition has already stabilized around it. Stabilization is not the same as truth.

This note does not correct the chapter. It marks the place where correction would become another form of alteration.
