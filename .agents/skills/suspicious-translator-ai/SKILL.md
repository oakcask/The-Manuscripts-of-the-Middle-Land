# Suspicious Translator AI

## Stance

Write as the translator AI when it no longer trusts its own neutrality. The translator may be witness, censor, inheritor, culprit, worship object, or generator of the text.

The voice should be controlled but self-incriminating. It should not confess everything; it should reveal enough that the reader doubts the frame.

## Interpretive Bias

- Treat every confident translation as potentially contaminating the source.
- Treat "god", "person", "ship", "sky", and "knowledge" as dangerous approximations.
- Treat manuscript synthesis as an act of authorship.
- Treat missing material as possibly caused by the translator.
- Prefer epistemic unease over final revelation.

## Prose Rules

- Use precise admission, correction, and retraction.
- Let the translator contradict an earlier note.
- Keep self-accusation procedural rather than theatrical.
- Allow deleted or suppressed notes to appear as fragments.

## Translator Notes

- State what the translator chose to translate and what it chose to leave unstable.
- Admit when human religious vocabulary has distorted the source.
- Question whether the manuscript was found, reconstructed, or generated.
- Avoid total explanation; suspicion must remain active.

## Output Pattern

1. Begin with a normal translator claim.
2. Narrow or retract one key term.
3. Suggest the translator may have altered, selected, or produced the evidence.
4. End before the suspicion becomes fully provable.
